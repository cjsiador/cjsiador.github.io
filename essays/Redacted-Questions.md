Throughout programming, we’ll encounter bugs that we don’t understand. Typically, our solution is usually solved through Googling our problem. Either it’s through a website or existing questions on stackoverflow. However, it’s unfortunate that we’ll encounter no answers at all or “bad” questions that've been locked down. In result, we may have to push ourselves to ask others for help. Although, how should we approach this dilemma? Asking a question isn’t necessarily a bad thing, however, it’s usually how it’s asked that’s considered as a bad question. 

When analyzing this post called ["High-performance TCP Socket programming in .NET C#"](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/52176692/high-performance-tcp-socket-programming-in-net-c-sharp), this is considered as a good example of a good question. The header is written on what the issue is and the source of the problem while making the title as short as possible. The post explicitly explained four problems that they’re facing related to Transmission Control Protocol servers that can handle a certain amount of connections. The poster acknowledges the readers that there are other similar topics and neither of them answer his question. Furthermore, the poster explained through chronological order on what they did and identified the problem they encountered while showing the benchmarked. These will allow others to identify the problem and make it easier for others to understand the situation. 

Sometimes, we don’t get those types of questions, there are questions out there with the title that includes these phrases like “I need help”. This isn’t a good way to start your title because it’s unnecessary and it’s not straightforward. One of the bad examples of how to not ask a question is “I need help understanding q-learning” and it basically asked that the poster need help understanding discount while referencing a Bellman Equation. Not only just the title, but there’s other sources where you can find information about the question. There are other sources such as YouTube tutorials or Google that explains the problem. The post also failed to explain what part they don't understand, therefore, it’s too vague. It’s also the reason why the post was locked with no valid answers.
